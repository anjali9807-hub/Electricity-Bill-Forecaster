# Abstract

Electricity consumption forecasting plays a significant role in energy management and cost optimization. The objective of this project is to develop an Electricity Bill Forecaster using Multiple Linear Regression to predict monthly electricity bills based on historical electricity usage data. The model considers various factors such as units consumed, average daily usage, number of occupants, air conditioner usage hours, and appliance count to estimate future electricity expenses accurately.

The dataset is preprocessed to remove inconsistencies and analyze relationships between variables through correlation analysis and visualization techniques. Multiple Linear Regression is then applied to identify the relationship between input features and electricity bill amounts. The dataset is divided into training and testing sets to evaluate the performance of the model. Evaluation metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R² Score are used to measure prediction accuracy.

The results demonstrate that the proposed model can effectively forecast electricity bills and identify the major factors influencing energy consumption. Such predictions can help households and organizations plan their budgets, monitor electricity usage, and implement energy-saving measures. The project highlights the practical application of machine learning techniques in solving real-world energy management problems while maintaining simplicity, interpretability, and computational efficiency.
